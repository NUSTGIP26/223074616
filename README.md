Spatial Analysis of Educational and Healthcare Accessibility in Namibia (2011–2023–2033).

INTRODUCTION Improving quality of life and promoting sustainable development depend heavily on having access to basic services like healthcare and education (Hansen, 1959).
Certain communities in Namibia may be underserved due to population expansion and unequal facility distribution. 
This project was created to use Geographic Information Systems (GIS) to analyze the accessibility of hospitals and schools throughout Namibia. 
Based on population growth projections,the study projects future conditions for 2033 and assesses accessibility for the years 2011 and 2023. 
Finding underserved areas and suggesting appropriate sites for additional facilities are the primary objectives.
PURPOSE OF THE PROJECT Recognize how successfully hospitals and schools serve the populace. Determine which places have inadequate service accessibility. 
Examine the impact of population expansion on accessibility. Estimate the demand for services in 2033.
Encourage improved decision-making and planning Creating an interactive application that lets users examine the outcomes graphically was another aspect of the project. 
Service Area Analysis A 20 km buffer was created around each school and hospital. 
Areas within 20 km → considered served and Areas beyond 20 km → considered underserved All buffers were merged into a single service area to simplify analysis.
5.4 Overlay Analysis The population layer was overlaid with the service area to determine: Which areas fall inside service coverage and Which areas fall outside.
This resulted in two categories: Served population and Underserved population. 
5.5 Priority Analysis A weighted scoring system was applied using: Population size (2023 and 2033) ,Population growth rate ,Distance to facilities and Distance to roads and towns . 
Each area was assigned a priority level: Very High ,High ,Moderate ,Low and High-priority areas represent locations with high demand and poor access.
5.6 Suggested Locations New facility locations were suggested by: Selecting high-priority underserved areas ,Ensuring locations are spatially distributed ,Avoiding clustering .
This ensures effective and realistic planning.
